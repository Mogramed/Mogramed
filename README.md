# Hi, I’m Mohamed Amine Khaldi 👋

I am an engineering student at CY Tech, specializing in applied mathematics, data science, and artificial intelligence, with a strong interest in software engineering, data systems, quantitative finance, and intelligent applications.

Throughout my journey, I have built a hybrid profile at the crossroads of engineering, data, AI, and product-oriented software development. I enjoy working on ambitious, practical, and meaningful projects that connect strong technical foundations with real business or user value.

Alongside my studies, I completed an apprenticeship at HSBC within Global Banking & Markets, in a demanding environment combining market finance, cloud technologies, automation, and data processing. I contributed to several high-value initiatives involving computation, technical and financial metrics monitoring, billing-related processes, workflow optimization, cloud integrations, and the development of Python and Java tools serving both technical and business teams. This experience strengthened not only my engineering discipline, but also my ability to industrialize solutions and operate in a large-scale production environment.

I also had the opportunity to complete an international academic and technical experience in Indonesia at Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS), in a research-oriented environment combining mathematics, computer science, and innovation. During this experience, I developed a finance chatbot from scratch, working on architecture, backend logic, tool integration, and user-facing product design. This project deepened my interest in intelligent systems, augmented assistants, and products that turn data into practical value.

Today, I am especially interested in the intersection of:
- artificial intelligence and decision systems,
- data science and model industrialization,
- backend and full-stack development,
- finance, risk management, and quantitative tools,
- cloud technologies and scalable architectures.

On this GitHub, I mainly share projects that reflect how I like to work: learn fast, build cleanly, connect technology to real use cases, and aim for projects with genuine ambition.

## Featured Projects

### 1. F-chat
A finance chatbot developed during my experience at ITS, designed as an intelligent application able to assist users on finance-related questions, analysis workflows, and data-driven interactions. This project gave me the opportunity to work on the full design of a product combining backend development, conversational logic, specialized tools, and application interface design.

### 2. VaR-project
A project focused on quantitative finance and risk management, centered on Value at Risk computation and financial scenario analysis. It reflects my strong interest in bridging applied mathematics, software engineering, APIs, visualization, and practical decision-support tools.

### 3. ChainTicket
An ambitious blockchain-based ticketing project designed to address real issues such as fraud, scalping, abusive resale, and post-event engagement. The idea is to build a programmable ticketing platform powered by NFTs, with a controlled secondary marketplace, anti-fraud logic, on-chain check-in, and the possibility of transforming tickets into digital assets or collectibles after the event. It is one of the projects that best reflects my desire to build innovative systems at the intersection of product thinking, technology, and economic design.

### 1. F-chat
Un chatbot financier développé durant mon expérience à l’ITS, pensé comme une application intelligente capable d’assister l’utilisateur sur des problématiques liées à la finance, à l’analyse et à l’exploitation de données. C’est probablement l’un des projets qui reflète le mieux ma volonté de créer des systèmes innovants à la frontière entre produit, technologie et modèle économique.
